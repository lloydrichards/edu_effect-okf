# Houseplant

A houseplant is a plant grown indoors for foliage, flowers, form, fragrance, collection value, or wellbeing. Most common houseplants are tropical or subtropical species adapted to stable temperatures and filtered light, while succulents and cacti evolved for brighter and drier conditions.

Houseplant success comes from matching a plant's native-adapted preferences to the indoor environment: [light intensity](/care/light-intensity.md), [watering](/care/watering.md), [relative humidity](/care/relative-humidity.md), [temperature range](/care/temperature-range.md), and [growing medium](/care/growing-medium.md).

## Core Relationships

* Every houseplant has a practical [plant difficulty](/foundations/plant-difficulty.md).
* Every houseplant creates or occupies a [microclimate](/foundations/microclimate.md).
* New houseplants need [acclimation](/foundations/acclimation.md) after purchase or relocation.
* Plant profiles combine requirements, risks, and common problems.
