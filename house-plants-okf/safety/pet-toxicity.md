# Pet Toxicity

Pet toxicity is a cross-cutting constraint when choosing houseplants. Toxicity should be checked at the specific plant or taxon level rather than inferred from common names.

## Use in Plant Selection

Some plants are toxic or irritating, some are considered pet-safer, and some vary by species or cultivar. Safety is part of [choosing-and-placing-a-plant](/workflows/choosing-and-placing-a-plant.md), but this page avoids linking every plant profile so pet safety does not become a graph super-hub.

## Emergency Guidance

If ingestion is suspected, contact a veterinarian or animal poison control service. The ASPCA Animal Poison Control plant database is a primary reference: https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants
