# Choosing and Placing a Plant

1. Measure available [light intensity](/care/light-intensity.md) through [window exposure](/care/window-exposure.md) or [artificial lighting](/care/artificial-lighting.md).
2. Check [temperature range](/care/temperature-range.md) risks such as vents, drafts, and cold glass.
3. Estimate [relative humidity](/care/relative-humidity.md) and whether a better [microclimate](/foundations/microclimate.md) is possible.
4. Match plant to [plant difficulty](/foundations/plant-difficulty.md).
5. Check [pet toxicity](/safety/pet-toxicity.md) before buying.
6. Inspect for pests and use [quarantine](/pests/quarantine.md).
7. Plan gradual [acclimation](/foundations/acclimation.md).

Good beginner candidates are plants whose profiles match the available light, moisture pattern, and safety constraints.
