# Window Exposure

Window exposure translates outdoor sunlight into an indoor plant placement decision. North, east, south, and west windows differ by hemisphere and local obstructions, but direction remains a useful first approximation.

## Practical Use

* North windows are often lower light.
* East windows often provide gentle morning sun.
* South and west windows can provide high light and heat.
* Distance from glass sharply reduces [light intensity](/care/light-intensity.md).

Use exposure as input to [choosing-and-placing-a-plant](/workflows/choosing-and-placing-a-plant.md), then monitor symptoms such as [leggy growth](/problems/leggy-growth.md) or [leaf scorch](/problems/leaf-scorch.md).
